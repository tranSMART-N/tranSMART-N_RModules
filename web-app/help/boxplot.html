A box plot is a compact way to show a distribution of values. The box shows the values between the 25th percentile (the lower quartile) and 75th percentile (the upper quartile), which is called the interquartile range. The line inside the box shows the median, and the point shows the mean. The two "whiskers" on either side of the box show the adjacent values, which represent the maximum and minimum points in the data.

If the adjacent values are no more than 1.5 times the length of the box, then the actual minimum (or maximum) values are plotted. Otherwise, whiskers are drawn at 1.5 times the length of the box. In the case where there are values that are outside that range, they are plotted as separate points, called the outside values.
